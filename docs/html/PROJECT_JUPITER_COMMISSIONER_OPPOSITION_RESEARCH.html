Accountability Issues - EXTENSIVE

#### 1. DOXXING INCIDENT (Sept 5, 2025)

What Happened:
  • Posted to Facebook: Name, home address, and email of at least one New Mexico resident opposing Jupiter
  • Posted lengthy list of names of individuals (many Doña Ana County residents) who submitted written public comment in opposition
  • Clear intent: Intimidation and retaliation
Why This Matters:
  • First Amendment Violation: Retaliation against residents for exercising free speech rights
  • Abuse of Office: Used position to access public comment records, then weaponized that access
  • Invasion of Privacy: Exposed personal information that could endanger residents
  • Creating Chilling Effect: Discourages future public participation (if you speak up, you'll be doxxed)
  • Potential Criminal Violation: Depending on NM law, could constitute harassment, intimidation, or misuse of public records
Official Response:
  • New Mexico Environmental Law Center sent formal letter on Sept 8, 2025 to:

- Commissioner Shannon Reynolds

- Doña Ana County Board of Commissioners

- County Management

  • NMELC demanded:

- Immediate action to ensure misconduct doesn't happen again

- Protection for community members participating in public forums

- Guarantee against retaliation, harassment, and threats to safety

Consequences for Reynolds:
  • NMELC stated: "We Will Defend the Constitutional Rights of Community Members to Submit Comments and Will Not Tolerate Intimidation Tactics"
  • Created documented record of misconduct
  • Establishes pattern of hostility toward Jupiter opponents

#### 2. Pattern of Pro-Jupiter Advocacy

  • Voted YES twice despite overwhelming community opposition
  • Doxxing indicates personal investment in Jupiter's approval (beyond normal commissioner duty)

#### 3. Violation of Public Trust

  • Commissioners are supposed to WELCOME public input, not punish it
