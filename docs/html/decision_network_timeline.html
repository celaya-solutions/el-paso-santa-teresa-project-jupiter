Communication Patterns and Red Flags

#### Pattern 1: State Pre-Commitment Timeline: Feb 25 (State MOU) → Sept 19 (County vote) Significance: State committed 7 months before local approval Implication: State pressure on county to approve project already blessed at state level Network: Governor's Office → NMED/State Engineer/EDD → County (via political channels, not documented) Red Flag: Did state officials pressure county commissioners? Phone logs and emails needed (IPRA request).
#### Pattern 2: Private-to-Public Transition Timeline: Early 2025 (NDA + private meetings) → Sept 17 (document release) → Sept 19 (vote) Significance: Months of private negotiations, then 2-day public review Implication: Decision made privately, public process was theater Network: BorderPlex ↔ County Commissioners/Staff (NDA shield) → Public (controlled release) Red Flag: Who attended private meetings? What was discussed? NDA prevents disclosure, but Open Meetings Act may void NDA for public officials conducting public business.
#### Pattern 3: Coordinated 4-1 Voting Bloc Timeline: Sept 19 (2 YES votes) + Oct 14 (1 YES vote) = 3 identical 4-1 outcomes Significance: Four commissioners voted identically three times Implication: Either coordinated decision-making OR shared pressure/incentive Network: Schaljo-Hernandez + Gameros + Reynolds + Sanchez (voting bloc) Red Flag: Did the four communicate outside public meetings to coordinate votes? If so, Open Meetings Act violation. Research Needed: Text messages, emails, phone logs among the four commissioners (IPRA request).
#### Pattern 4: Retaliation Against Opposition Timeline: Community protests → Reynolds doxxing (Sept 5) → YES votes (Sept 19, Oct 14) Significance: Reynolds targeted opponents, then voted for project they opposed Implication: Intimidation to suppress dissent + personal stake in project approval Network: Community opponents → Reynolds (hostile response) → BorderPlex (vote support) Red Flag: Did Reynolds have undisclosed connections to BorderPlex or financial interest in project approval?
#### Pattern 5: Document Version Control Issues Timeline: Oct 1 (initial docs, omissions) → Oct 12 (updated docs with water caps and $6.9M) Significance: Key commitments added or disclosed after vote Implication: Either post-vote document modification OR selective initial disclosure Network: County Staff/BorderPlex (document preparation) → Public (controlled release) Red Flag: Were documents altered after Sept 19 vote? Who authorized changes? Forensic document analysis needed (metadata, version history).
#### Pattern 6: Synchronization of Execution and Lawsuit Timeline: Oct 17 (lawsuit filed) = Oct 17 (Community Development Agreement signed) Significance: County executed another agreement on the same day lawsuit was filed Implication: Rush to finalize binding commitments despite legal challenge Network: County + BorderPlex (execution) | Plaintiffs (legal challenge) Red Flag: Did county rush execution to create additional legal barriers to unwinding the deal?

KEY FINDINGS

1. State Pre-Empted Local Control

The Governor's Feb 25 MOU created fait accompli pressure on county decision. State committed regulatory fast-tracking before county vote.

2. Closed-Door Decision-Making

NDA + private meetings + 2-day document release = Decision made privately, public process was formality.

3. Coordinated Voting Bloc

Four commissioners voted identically three times. Coordination suggests either shared agenda or shared pressure.

4. Retaliation Pattern

Reynolds doxxed opponents, then voted for project. Intimidation + support = evidence of personal stake or external pressure.

5. Document Control Issues

Version discrepancies and late releases suggest either post-vote modifications or strategic withholding of key terms.

6. Municipal Exclusion

Sunland Park Mayor excluded despite project proximity. County bypassed affected municipality's input.
