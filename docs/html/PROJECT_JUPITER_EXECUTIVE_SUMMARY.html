PROJECT JUPITER OPPOSITION

Executive Summary for Officials and Media

Prepared: January 22, 2026 Location: El Paso, TX & Santa Teresa/Sunland Park, NM Project: Project Jupiter - $165 Billion Data Center Complex

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Project Jupiter is a $165 billion hyperscale data center complex proposed for Santa Teresa, New Mexico, that poses severe threats to the health, safety, and economic wellbeing of predominantly Hispanic, low-income communities in Santa Teresa and Sunland Park, NM, and neighboring El Paso, TX.

A lawsuit was filed on October 17, 2025 by the New Mexico Environmental Law Center on behalf of community residents and organizations, challenging the unlawful approval of this project by the Doña Ana County Board of Commissioners.

CRITICAL FACTS

The Project

  • $165 BILLION in Industrial Revenue Bonds approved
  • Hyperscale data center complex with natural gas power plants, microgrid, battery storage, and desalination plant
  • Led by STACK Infrastructure and BorderPlex Digital Assets, LLC
  • Approved in 4-1 vote on September 19, 2025 despite massive community opposition

The Community at Risk

  • 91% Hispanic population
  • 52% low-income residents
  • Communities of Santa Teresa and Sunland Park, NM; adjacent to El Paso, TX
  • Already suffering from contaminated water - arsenic levels above federal safety limits
  • Decades of inadequate infrastructure and environmental burdens

WHY PROJECT JUPITER MUST BE STOPPED

1. LEGAL VIOLATIONS - Unlawful Approval Process

The Evidence:
  • County Commissioners approved project based on incomplete application with blank and missing pages
  • No environmental impact assessment conducted
  • No economic impact analysis provided to public
  • Closed-door meetings between county officials and developers, including Non-Disclosure Agreements signed months before public notification
  • Community residents "remained largely unaware of the proposed project up until days before the Board's decision"
The Law Violated:

State and local law requires county commissioners to make fully-informed decisions based on complete information. The approval was unlawful and procedurally deficient.

2. WATER CRISIS - Community Already Lacks Safe Drinking Water

Current Conditions:
  • Santa Teresa and Sunland Park have lacked access to clean, safe, affordable drinking water for decades
  • Arsenic contamination above federal health standards in current water supply
  • EPA inspection (2025) revealed:

- 3 of 4 arsenic treatment plants offline and bypassed for over a year

- Widespread neglect and disrepair of water utility facilities

- Unsafe drinking water being delivered to homes

Project Jupiter's Impact:
  • Data centers require massive water consumption for cooling operations
  • Project includes desalination plant - indicating enormous water demand
  • Will place immense additional strain on already failing water infrastructure
  • Communities cannot survive further depletion of water resources
Resident Testimony:
"We filed this lawsuit because the community's health is in danger. I'm protecting water rights for the community. Why would commissioners approve Project Jupiter without having all the documents? How can we live without water? Water is life."
— José Saldaña Jr., Sunland Park resident

3. AIR POLLUTION - Natural Gas Power Plants

The Threat:

Project Jupiter requires massive electricity generation, necessitating:

  • Natural gas microgrid power plants:

- West Microgrid: 4.1 miles southwest of Santa Teresa

- East Microgrid: 3.6 miles south of Santa Teresa

  • Air pollution permit applications filed with NM Environment Department by Acoma, LLC
Community Impact:
  • Increased air, noise, light, water, and soil pollution
  • Health hazards for residents already bearing disproportionate environmental burdens
  • No adequate health impact assessment conducted

4. INTIMIDATION OF RESIDENTS

The Incident:

On September 5, 2025, Doña Ana County Commissioner Shannon Reynolds:

  • Publicly exposed on Facebook the name, home address, and email address of a resident opposing Project Jupiter
  • Posted a lengthy list of names of residents who submitted public comments opposing the project
  • Clear attempt to intimidate and silence community opposition
NMELC Response:

Sent formal demand letter stating this "gross misconduct" violates constitutional rights of community members to participate in public forums without fear of retaliation, harassment, or safety threats from elected officials.

5. ENVIRONMENTAL JUSTICE VIOLATION

The Pattern:
  • 91% Hispanic, 52% low-income community
  • Already suffering from:

- Contaminated drinking water (arsenic)

- Failed water infrastructure

- Inadequate public services

- Decades of environmental neglect

New Burden:

Project Jupiter adds:

  • Massive water consumption
  • Air pollution from power plants
  • Strain on electricity grid
  • Increased living costs (water rates, electricity rates)
  • Health and safety risks
This is Environmental Racism:

Vulnerable minority community forced to bear environmental harms for private corporate profit, without meaningful community consent or adequate environmental protections.

6. ECONOMIC HARM TO RESIDENTS

Tax Issues:
  • $165 billion in Industrial Revenue Bonds with Payment in Lieu of Taxes (PILOT) structure
  • Reduces county property tax revenue that would normally fund public services
  • LEDA funding approved to pay Project Jupiter permit fees - taxpayer money supporting private development
Community Costs:
  • Increased water rates (already high, water contaminated)
  • Increased electricity rates (strain on grid)
  • Infrastructure deterioration
  • Health costs from pollution exposure
  • No demonstrated benefit to local residents
Missing Analysis:
"There's a missing economic analysis, there's a missing environmental analysis, and certainly, there's a missing community voice"
— Daisy Maldonado, Director, Empowerment Congress of Doña Ana County

7. LACK OF TRANSPARENCY

Secret Dealings:
  • County staff and officials engaged in closed-door meetings with developers for months
  • Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) signed with Project Jupiter companies earlier in 2025
  • Community kept in dark until days before approval vote
Inadequate Public Notice:
  • Notices only posted online - communities have limited internet access
  • No notices in Spanish despite 91% Hispanic population
  • Physical locations in impacted communities not used for notification
  • Even Sunland Park Mayor Javier Perea stated: "We're finding out information at the same time that press releases are being released"
Result:
"Our voices were still not heard"
— Vivian Fuller, Santa Teresa resident

THE LAWSUIT

Filed: October 17, 2025 Court: Third Judicial District Court, Doña Ana County, NM Plaintiffs:
  • Empowerment Congress of Doña Ana County
  • José Saldaña Jr., Sunland Park resident
  • Vivian Fuller, Santa Teresa resident
Legal Representation:

New Mexico Environmental Law Center (NMELC)

Ordinances Challenged:
  • Ordinance 367-2025: $165 billion Industrial Revenue Bonds
  • Ordinance 368-2025: LEDA funding for project development
  • Related LEDA ordinance: Funding for anticipated permit fees
Legal Claims:
  • Violation of state and local laws requiring fully-informed decisions
  • Procedurally deficient approval based on incomplete applications
  • Failure to conduct required environmental and economic analyses
  • Inadequate public notice and community engagement
  • Unlawful approval process
Vote Record:
  • September 19, 2025: 4-1 approval (IRB and first LEDA ordinance)
  • October 14, 2025: 4-1 approval (second LEDA ordinance)
  • Commissioner Susana Chaparro voted NO both times - "remaining steadfast in her commitment to Doña Ana County constituents"

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR EL PASO

While Project Jupiter is located in Doña Ana County, NM, its impacts directly affect El Paso, TX:

  • Shared Water Resources: Rio Grande aquifer serves both regions
  • Air Quality: Pollution from microgrid power plants crosses state lines
  • Electricity Grid: Regional grid strain affects all borderland communities
  • Economic Precedent: Sets dangerous precedent for similar projects in El Paso area
  • Community Connection: Santa Teresa/Sunland Park residents are part of greater El Paso borderland community
El Paso residents have a stake in opposing Project Jupiter.

BOTTOM LINE

Project Jupiter is unlawfully approved, environmentally destructive, and fundamentally unjust.

A predominantly Hispanic, low-income community already suffering from contaminated water and failed infrastructure is being forced to accept a $165 billion project that will:

  • Deplete scarce water resources in a community that already lacks safe drinking water
  • Pollute the air with natural gas power plants
  • Provide no meaningful benefit to local residents
  • Enrich private corporations at the expense of public health and safety
The approval was rushed, secretive, and based on incomplete information.

County officials violated the law, silenced community voices, and even intimidated residents who dared to oppose the project.

This must be stopped.

The lawsuit provides a legal path to overturn the unlawful approval. Community organizing, media attention, and political pressure can ensure Project Jupiter never breaks ground.

Water is life. The community's health is in danger. We must act now.
