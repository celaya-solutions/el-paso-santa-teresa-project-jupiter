OPPO RESEARCH METHODS

Public Records Requests (IPRA)

File requests for:

  • All emails/communications between commissioners and BorderPlex (Feb-Oct 2025)
  • All emails mentioning "Project Jupiter," "BorderPlex," or "data center"
  • Campaign finance reports (NM SOS)
  • Conflict of interest disclosure forms
  • Meeting attendance and voting records

Database Searches

  • NM Secretary of State: Business entities, campaign finance
  • County property records: Real estate holdings near project site
  • Court records: Any litigation involving commissioners
  • Federal Election Commission: Federal campaign contributions (if applicable)

Media Archive Review

  • Local newspaper archives (Las Cruces Sun-News, El Paso Times, etc.)
  • TV news archives (KFOX14, etc.)
  • Social media (Facebook, Twitter/X) - use Wayback Machine for deleted content
  • Commission meeting videos (county website archives)

Source Interviews

  • Journalists who covered Project Jupiter
  • Community activists who attended meetings
  • County staff (off the record) about internal dynamics
  • Commissioner Chaparro (explain why she voted NO)

ETHICAL GUIDELINES FOR OPPO RESEARCH

  • Stick to Public Records: No hacking, no dumpster diving, no illegal surveillance
  • Verify Everything: Two independent sources for any claim
  • Context Matters: Don't cherry-pick quotes; represent positions fairly
  • Focus on Official Conduct: Personal life is off-limits unless directly relevant to public duties
  • Cite Sources: Every claim must have a verifiable source
