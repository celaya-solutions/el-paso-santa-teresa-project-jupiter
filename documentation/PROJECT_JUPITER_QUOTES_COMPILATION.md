## LAWSUIT DOCUMENTATION QUOTES

### From Press Release (October 23, 2025):

**On IRB Ordinance:**
> "The first ordinance authorizes Doña Ana County to issue $165 billion dollars of Industrial Revenue Bonds (IRBs) to support Project Jupiter."

**On LEDA Funding:**
> "The second and third ordinances authorize the issuance of New Mexico Local Economic Development Act (LEDA) funding to support the development of Project Jupiter."

**On Public Hearing:**
> "At a contentious public hearing on September 19th, the Doña Ana County Board voted 4-1 to adopt the IRB ordinance and the first LEDA ordinance."

**On Closed-Door Meetings:**
> "Doña Ana County staff and officials had been engaging in discussions and closed-door meetings with the project developers months in advance, even entering into a Non-Disclosure Agreement with the Project Jupiter companies earlier this year."

**On Lack of Public Awareness:**
> "Community residents remained largely unaware of the proposed project up until days before the Board's decision."

## AIR QUALITY PERMIT DOCUMENTATION QUOTES

### From NMELC Letter to NMED (December 15, 2025):

**On Public Interest:**
> "We strongly urge notice of the complete applications be broadly disseminated, in both English and Spanish, at physical locations in the impacted communities of Sunland Park and Santa Teresa, due to the important and significant public interest in this microgrid power plant project and its associated Project Jupiter."

**On Internet Access Barriers:**
> "Community members often do not have reliable internet access, and many may not know to check the Department's website."

**On Microgrid Facilities:**
Referencing "two air pollution permit applications for the following facilities related to Project Jupiter:
- West Microgrid: 4.1 miles southwest of Santa Teresa
- East Microgrid: 3.6 miles south of Santa Teresa"

## DOXXING INCIDENT DOCUMENTATION QUOTES

### From NMELC Statement (September 8, 2025):

**On Commissioner Reynolds' Actions:**
> "On Friday, September 5, 2025, Doña Ana County Commissioner Shannon Reynolds made a public post on Facebook exposing the name, home address, and email address of a New Mexico resident, seemingly in an effort to intimidate the person based on their opposition to Project Jupiter."

**On List of Names:**
> "Additionally, Commissioner Reynolds posted a lengthy list of names of individuals, many of whom are Doña Ana County residents, that have submitted written public comment to the County in opposition to Project Jupiter."

## OFFICIAL PROJECT DESCRIPTION

### From Doña Ana County Website:

**Project Overview:**
> "Project Jupiter is a large-scale data center campus proposed for development in Doña Ana County, in Santa Teresa, New Mexico. The project is being led by STACK Infrastructure in partnership with BorderPlex Digital Assets. With an estimated total investment of up to $165,000,000,000 it represents one of the most significant private-sector infrastructure initiatives in the region's history."
